Kénitra — Complete Neighbourhood Guide

Eight Zones.
One City to Understand.

Kénitra's real estate market is not uniform. A 40–60% premium separates Centre Ville from the urban periphery — and within each zone, sub-neighbourhoods carry their own pricing logic. This guide maps all eight zones with current prices, rental benchmarks, Daam Sakani eligibility, and LGV access times.

At a glance

8 residential zones, 26 sub-neighbourhoods, one complete map.

Buy prices range from 5,500 MAD/m² for older second-hand stock in peripheral zones to 15,000 MAD/m² for premium haut-standing new builds in Centre Ville. The LGV station sits inside Centre Ville — every distance below is measured from there.

City Overview

Why Geography Matters
More Than Price Per m².

Kénitra sits 40 km north of Rabat on the Atlantic coast. The LGV high-speed rail connects it to Rabat in 14 minutes and Casablanca in 55 minutes, making it one of the most accessible secondary cities in Morocco for professionals and investors. But the city's internal geography is sharply differentiated: Centre Ville trades at a significant premium to the peripheral zones, the coastal strip runs on seasonal logic, and the outer growth belt offers the lowest entry points in the market. Within each zone, individual sub-neighbourhoods carry their own pricing micro-logic.

Buy prices range from 5,500 MAD/m² for older second-hand stock in peripheral zones to 15,000 MAD/m² for premium haut-standing new builds in Centre Ville. The two tables below summarise all 26 sub-neighbourhoods — buy prices first, then monthly rent benchmarks. Use the anchor links above to jump to any zone's full analysis.

Zone 01 — Premium Core

Centre Ville Region

Maamora Mimosas La Ville Haute Val Fleuri El Mellah Campus Universitaire
⬡ LGV station — 5–12 min walk

Centre Ville is Kénitra's historic, civic, and commercial core — and the price anchor of the entire market. The LGV station sits here, walkable from every sub-neighbourhood. Daily services, administration, banking, and retail are all on foot. This is where demand consistently outpaces supply for quality units and where resale liquidity is highest in the city.

Maamora

The strongest residential area within Centre Ville. Maamora is close to the LGV station, the city's main commercial streets, and all central services. It sits at the upper end of Kénitra's residential market. Strong rental appeal due to centrality — tenants who want everything on foot gravitate here, keeping vacancy rates low and rents supported.

Zone 02 — Growth Corridor

Kasbah Mehdia Region

Addoha Alliance Kasbah Mehdia
⬡ LGV 18–25 min drive

This zone has changed dramatically over two decades. New roads now connect the Alliance-Addoha axis to the Route Nationale and autoroute, and what was once a remote peripheral area has grown into a largely self-contained residential corridor with schools, shops, businesses, and regular public transport.

Addoha

Socio-economic and government-subsidised low-income housing — the original Addoha wave that built out this corridor from the early 2000s. It is now a fully established community with schools, shops, businesses, and public transport. The original social housing stock trades at very low prices in the used market, making it one of the most accessible entry points in Kénitra. Daam Sakani Tier 1 and Tier 2 apply to used apartments within the price range.

Large socio-economic blocks that have grown dramatically since the early phases. Now includes mid-range new developments, villa zones, and a mall — giving it a more complete urban character than the purely residential Addoha fabric. Kasbah Mehdia

A hill overlooking the beach, built around the ancient kasbah. Since the 1990s, development via Al Omrane plots has produced a mix of R+2 and R+3 houses, villas, and older fabric. The character is entirely different from the tower-block homogeneity of Addoha and Alliance — more organic, lower density, with the ancient fortification as a backdrop and the Atlantic coast nearby.

Zone 03 — Coastal Lifestyle

Mehdia Beach

Atlantic-facing strip Beachfront residences Plage Mehdia
⬡ LGV 25–35 min drive

Mehdia Beach is the Atlantic-facing strip — a seasonal and second-home market driven by short-term rental investors rather than primary residents or LGV commuters. Prime new stock starts from 12,000 MAD/m², with some coastal units going higher. The second-hand market varies widely depending on distance from the waterfront, floor, and building age.

The seasonal rental logic here is strong: a well-positioned apartment can achieve peak summer returns that are multiples of its annual long-let value. Daam Sakani's five-year primary residence obligation creates a structural tension with short-term rental strategies — eligible units exist, but the subsidy is difficult to use alongside a seasonal rental operation.

Monthly rent (long-let)3,650–5,000+ MAD
Daam Tier 2 — select units; primary residence obligation conflicts with short-term rental

Second-home buyers, short-term rental investors, and MRE buyers wanting a coastal retreat.

Zone 04 — Southern Belt

South Kénitra

Bir Rami Ouest Bir Rami Est Jennan
⬡ LGV 15–22 min drive

The southern belt combines villa areas, apartment zones, and southbound growth toward Rabat. Three distinct sub-neighbourhoods with different price profiles — from the prestigious villa territory of Bir Rami Ouest to the growth-corridor logic of Jennan.

Bir Rami Ouest

The most prestigious and desirable residential address in South Kénitra. Larger plots, mature tree-lined streets, and established villas. This is primarily a villa market — buyers here are acquiring detached or semi-detached properties with gardens, not apartment blocks. New supply is effectively zero; acquisition is of the existing fabric. Daam Sakani does not generally apply to the villa market here.

Oulad Oujih

A large established residential zone west of the city centre. Mixed houses, villas, and apartments — some social housing stock, some newer private development. Oulad Oujih has a well-rooted community character and is well-served by local commerce and schools. Daam Sakani Tier 1 and Tier 2 units are available for lower-priced units within the stock.

Zone 06 — Most Heterogeneous

East Kénitra

Al Montazah Ancienne Medina Khabazat Hay Salam Hay Errabii Hay Nahda Hay Al Amal
⬡ LGV 10–20 min drive

East Kénitra is the city's most heterogeneous residential fabric. From the green, modern Al Montazah to the dense traditional life of the Ancienne Medina and Khabazat, to the established hay chaabi neighbourhoods — every buyer profile from value investors to urban-texture seekers finds something here.

Al Montazah

A relatively recent neighbourhood positioned between the old medina, the city centre, and the Maamora forest. New apartment blocks, villas, green spaces, a public garden, schools, restaurants, and cafés give Al Montazah a noticeably greener and more open character than most eastern sub-zones. It is one of the more desirable eastern addresses and has attracted both family buyers and rental investors. Daam Sakani Tier 2 applies to qualifying new-build units.

Frequently Asked Questions
About Kénitra Neighbourhoods.

For capital appreciation and resale liquidity, Centre Ville has historically outperformed all other zones. Its LGV adjacency, commercial concentration, and limited supply of quality new stock sustain premium pricing. For gross rental yield, West and South-East Kénitra can produce 6–8% on entry-level stock — but liquidity at exit is significantly lower. Most investment-grade buyers should prioritise Centre Ville or South Kénitra (Bir Rami) for the balance of yield and liquidity.

Kasbah Mehdia Region (Addoha, Alliance), West Kénitra, South-East Kénitra, and North Kénitra have the widest selection of Daam Sakani Tier 1 units (≤300,000 MAD). For Tier 2 (300,001–700,000 MAD), South Kénitra (Bir Rami) and East Kénitra (Al Montazah, Hay Nahda) offer the most recently permitted qualifying inventory. Centre Ville has qualifying units at the smaller-size end but supply is more limited.

The LGV station's location in Centre Ville is one of the primary structural reasons for the zone's price premium. Buyers who commute to Rabat (14 min) or Casablanca (55 min) by LGV are concentrated in Centre Ville and immediate surrounds. Zones beyond 20 minutes' drive to the station — West, South-East, North — are less affected by LGV demand and trade at larger discounts to Centre Ville. For investment purposes, LGV-adjacent zones have historically shown stronger price resilience during market softening.

Yes — foreign nationals can purchase property in Mehdia Beach and all other Kénitra zones without restriction. The standard foreign buyer process applies: purchase must be funded through a CIM account (compte en devises ou compte en dirhams convertibles) opened at a Moroccan bank, the transfer must be documented at the notary, and conversion documentation enables full repatriation of sale proceeds under the investment code. Foreign buyers are not eligible for Daam Sakani, which is reserved for Moroccan nationals.

Bir Rami Ouest is the older and more established of the two sub-zones, with more retail development, better road infrastructure, and slightly higher resale values. Bir Rami Est is newer, with more uniform mid-rise apartment blocks, lower prices, and fewer immediate amenities — but newer construction quality and a larger proportion of post-2023 permitted stock. For Daam Sakani buyers, Bir Rami Est may offer more qualifying units at better prices; for resale-focused buyers, Bir Rami Ouest has the more established market.

For MRE buyers using the Daam Sakani bail gratuit arrangement (placing a parent, child, or spouse in the property free of charge to satisfy the 5-year condition), the most cost-effective zones are Kasbah Mehdia Region and South Kénitra, where Tier 2 units in the 400,000–650,000 MAD range are most abundant. Centre Ville is a strong choice if capital appreciation is the primary objective and the family member will benefit from the LGV connectivity. West and South-East Kénitra suit MRE buyers with tight budgets targeting Tier 1 Daam Sakani.
