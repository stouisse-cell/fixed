Why Kénitra

A connected city.
A more strategic entry point.

For many buyers, Kénitra offers something increasingly difficult to find in larger Moroccan cities: stronger value at entry, efficient connectivity, and a lifestyle that feels both practical and livable.

Today, residents already benefit from high-speed rail access to Rabat (approximately 25 minutes), Casablanca (approximately 1 hour 10 minutes), and Tangier (approximately 45 minutes), while enjoying significantly more accessible pricing than comparable properties in the capital.

Looking ahead, Morocco’s planned LGV expansion could further strengthen Kénitra’s long-term position. Once completed, projected rail connections could place Marrakech approximately 1 hour 30 minutes away and Mohammed V International Airport approximately 45 minutes away by train, further integrating Kénitra into Morocco’s major economic, tourism, and transportation corridors.

While appreciation can never be guaranteed, infrastructure upgrades of this scale have historically had a meaningful impact on surrounding property markets. As Kénitra becomes increasingly connected to major economic hubs while remaining more accessible than Rabat and Casablanca, many buyers see meaningful long-term upside in entering the market earlier.

Beyond connectivity, the city continues to attract families, professionals, returning diaspora buyers, and long-term investors drawn to its proximity to the Atlantic coast, improving infrastructure, and more accessible entry points compared to larger markets. Elaf Sky View places buyers in one of the city’s most established districts, offering a combination of location quality, connectivity, and finish that can be difficult to find at similar price points elsewhere in the Rabat-Salé region.
